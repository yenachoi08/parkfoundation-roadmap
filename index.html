Evolution Strategy
Phase 1

Listening & Discovery

Institutional Knowledge CaptureDocumenting current workflows to ensure the new system protects the team's existing expertise.
Collaborative VettingInvolving staff in demos to find the tool that feels most intuitive for their daily work.
Gap AnalysisIdentifying where manual tasks can be simplified to free up staff time for mission-focused work.
Phase 2

Sustainable Build

Data Integrity ScrubCleaning legacy records before the move, ensuring a "fresh start" for the entire organization.
Finance PartnershipWorking with the CFO to automate data flows to Sage Intacct, reducing administrative friction.
Automated ComplianceEmbedding IRS status checks into the workflow to simplify audit readiness.
Phase 3

Earned Adoption

The Sandbox EnvironmentA safe space for staff to explore the new tools without affecting live data or reporting.
Tailored TrainingVideo guides and SOPs built around Park Foundation’s specific, unique grant cycles.
Continuous OptimizationOngoing clinics to refine the system based on staff feedback after launch.

Our Shared Philosophy

Building Capacity, Preserving Knowledge

A system transition at Park Foundation isn't about replacing what works; it's about evolving the tools to better serve the mission. We prioritize organizational trust and process clarity. Our goal is to ensure the technology eventually becomes invisible, allowing the team to focus on the programmatic work they do best.

Co-AuthorshipStaff are part of the design process, not just recipients of a new tool.
Data StewardshipWe clean and validate all legacy data to ensure the new foundation is rock solid.
Sustainable AdoptionTraining is personalized and ongoing, ensuring no one is left behind in the transition.

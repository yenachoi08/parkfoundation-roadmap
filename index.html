Our Journey Map
Phase 1 · Listening First
Discovery & Alignment
Ensuring the tool fits the team
Capturing institutional wisdom
Before any decisions are made, I want to learn from each of you. The expertise you hold across these 8 funding areas is what makes Park special; the new system must respect and protect that legacy.
Respecting your quarterly rhythm
Transitioning shouldn't be a burden. We’ll map out our work to ensure heavy technical tasks happen during quieter periods, leaving you focused when Board dockets are due.
Simplifying the path for grantees
By properly mapping the LOI-to-proposal bridge, we reduce the amount of re-entry for your partners. It’s about building trust by showing we value their time as much as yours.
Our partnership: Your daily experience with the system is what will guide our selection—not a generic sales checklist.
Phase 2 · Caring for our Foundation
Sustainable Build
Technical setup done with care
Getting fiscal sponsorship right
These relationships are complex. We’ll build structures that accurately reflect the 501(c)(3) of record while keeping your focus on the actual project leads you support.
Collaborative sync with Finance
I want to work directly with Roberta to ensure Sage Intacct integration is rock solid. We want to reach a place where her team has data they trust with zero manual re-entry.
Compliance that stays out of your way
By building hard-stop IRS checks directly into the workflow, we automate the "policing" of the data so you can stay focused on the relational side of grantmaking.
Maintaining continuity: We’ll keep the current system live as our safety net until the new build has proven its reliability.
Phase 3 · Growing Together
Training & Support
Confidence and continuous improvement
YourCause portal optimization
The 2025 transition is a great foundation. We’ll keep smoothing out the grantee portal so that every interaction feels seamless and supportive for returning partners.
Digital logs for verbal updates
If a phone call is more helpful than a report, we should support that. We’ll create simple ways to log verbal narratives so your relationships stay personal, but your records stay complete.
Collaborative adoption clinics
We’ll use low-pressure sandbox sessions to explore the new tools. Your feedback in these sessions will be the driver for how we keep refining and evolving the system.

Reflections from my experience with GMS transitions
🤝
People matter more than platforms
I’ve seen the best software fail because staff didn’t feel heard during the build. My priority is ensuring you feel like co-authors of this change, so the tool actually supports your expertise rather than getting in its way.
⚖️
The pace should feel right for you
Trust is the bedrock of trust-based philanthropy, and that applies internally too. I won’t rush a go-live date if the team doesn’t feel ready. Sustainable adoption happens at the speed of conversation, not just technical milestones.
🌱
The tool should eventually be invisible
The ultimate success of this roadmap is reaching a place where you stop thinking about the database and start focusing entirely on the mission. We want a system that works for you, not one you work for.

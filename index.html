Realistic implementation timeline — 12 to 18 months
A note on timing: Choosing a grants management system typically takes 2–3 months of evaluation alone. After selection, data cleaning and migration can take 6–12 months. This roadmap reflects that reality with work streams running in tandem so the organization makes progress on every front without waiting for one step to finish before the next begins. The current system, YourCause, stays live throughout.

Phase breakdown
Phase 1 · Months 1–4 (in tandem)
Discovery & Alignment
Requirements & selection
Institutional memory capture
Before anything else, I want to sit with each program area and just listen. The expertise in this building — across all 8 funding priorities — is exactly what the new system needs to reflect. No technology replaces that knowledge; it should serve it.
Quarterly cycle synchronization
Park's 10–12 week Board review rhythm is a real constraint, and I'd rather plan around it from day one than ask the team to absorb a system transition during docket season. We'll schedule everything with that in mind.
LOI-to-proposal bridge mapping
I've set up this data flow in Temelio and Fluxx, and I know how clunky it gets when it's not properly mapped. Getting this right means applicants aren't re-entering the same information twice, and staff aren't cleaning it up on the back end.
GMS evaluation runs in parallel: The conversations with staff directly shape which platform we even consider — we're not selecting a system and then fitting Park into it.
Phase 2 · Months 5–12 (in tandem)
Sustainable Build
Technical setup & integration
Fiscal sponsorship logic
Fiscal sponsorship records are genuinely tricky to get right — I've built these structures in Fluxx and AkoyaGO and know the shortcuts that cause problems later. We'll configure it properly so program staff aren't holding workarounds together with spreadsheets.
Sage Intacct & portfolio sync
The field-mapping between a GMS and Sage Intacct is where most integrations quietly fail. I've been through this before, and I'd want to work through it directly with Roberta so what comes out the other end is something her team can actually trust — not another source of reconciliation headaches.
Automated compliance gates
Rather than relying on people to remember the right checks at the right time, I'll build IRS status and 501(c)(3) verification directly into the workflow as hard stops. I've done this in Fluxx and Temelio — it's one of those things that quietly saves a lot of headaches.
Both systems stay live: Nothing gets turned off until the new system has been tested, validated, and trusted by the people using it. No surprises.
Phase 3 · Months 9–18 (ongoing)
Training & Support
Adoption & refinement
YourCause environment optimization
The August 2025 transition is still fresh for returning grantees. I know the YourCause environment well enough to smooth out the rough edges — the goal is making it feel seamless for the people Park has long-standing relationships with.
Verbal reporting digital logs
Program Officers shouldn't have to translate a phone call into a written narrative just to satisfy the system. I'll build a simple, structured way to log verbal updates directly — it keeps the record clean without adding work to already full plates.
Earned adoption clinics
Monthly sandbox sessions, but low-pressure and practical — staff work through actual scenarios from their own grant portfolios. The feedback from those sessions is what shapes how the system keeps improving. Nobody gets left behind.

GMS evaluation framework — running parallel to Phase 1
I make it a priority to stay abreast of the evolving GMS landscape, including hands-on sandbox exploration of platforms like AkoyaGO, Fluxx, GivingData, and Temelio. This technical curiosity means I look past the sales demos to see how these systems actually handle the 'real world'—specifically the complexities of fiscal sponsorships, 330+ grantee partners, and quarterly Board cycles. The criteria below reflect the technical standards I’d be looking for on Park's behalf, intended as a starting point to be sharpened by staff conversations in Phase 1.

A few things I've learned along the way — from past GMS transitions
🗓️
These things always take longer than anyone thinks
Every transition I've been through ran longer than the original estimate. That's not a failure — it's just the reality of doing it carefully. Being honest about that upfront protects the team from feeling like they're falling behind when they're actually just doing it right.
🧹
Reducing the Burden of Process
In a trust-based model, technology should absorb administrative labor so our partners don't have to. By automating repetitive 'chores'—like the LOI-to-proposal bridge and compliance checks—we return valuable time to both our grantee partners and our staff.
⚖️🧹
Running two systems is hard, but skipping steps are harder
Technical transitions are successful only when they earn the team's trust. We prioritize a proactive data scrub to honor historical records, followed by a parallel testing period where the new system operates alongside the old. This 'safety net' approach ensures that the $30M+ annual grantmaking remains seamless and secure from start to finish.
